How the Model Works

A Random Forest is an ensemble of decision trees, each trained on a random bootstrap of the data and a random subset of features. To classify a new EEG window, every tree casts a vote, and the forest averages those votes into a probability score. Seizure-prone windows tend to have:

  • Higher signal energy — large amplitude rhythmic discharges
  • Increased low-frequency (delta/theta) power — slow waves dominate
  • Lower entropy — the signal becomes more rhythmic and predictable
  • Higher variance — sustained departure from baseline

The forest learns thresholds across these features and combines them into a probability between 0 and 1 for each window.
